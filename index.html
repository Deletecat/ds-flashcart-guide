DS Flashcard guide

Welcome to the DS Flashcard guide! This is a project started by me, Deletecat, which should hopefully help to set up your DS flashcard to play games and use Homebrew!

If you would like to help contribute to this site, click here to visit the Github Repository for the site!

Getting started

To get started, all you need is your flashcard, a compatible micro SD card, and a few minutes of your time. These carts do not take a long time to set up, though with some carts you may have to try a few kernels until you find one that works!

If you are looking to buy a cart, see r/flashcarts' quick start guide: https://www.reddit.com/r/flashcarts/wiki/ds-quick-start-guide - make sure to read through all of the cart descriptions to choose the right one for your console.

If you are on a DSi or 3DS, consider installing custom firmware (CFW) instead:

  • https://dsi.cfw.guide - Guide for installing Twilight Menu ++ and Unlaunch
  • https://3ds.hacks.guide - Guide for installing Luma3ds - Download Twilight menu ++ from universal updater after installation
If you get an error message (An error has occurred...) when trying to use your cart, this should fix it! CFW is free and easy to install, I would highly recommend doing so

SD Cards

Most DS Carts are SDHC compatible, there are some carts that only support up to 2 GB micro SD cards - like the original R4

For carts that only support 2 GB micro SD cards, you should see this message on their page:

This cart only supports micro SD cards up to 2 GB in capacity

Your micro SD card has to be formatted as Fat32 in order for it to work on these DS carts. Carts that do not have the 2 GB limit can use micro SD cards that are up to 2 TB in capacity when formatted correctly. Though, personally, anything above 32 GB is excessive. DS ROMs are small, so you do not need massive micro SD cards

! > Warning about Fake/Cheap Micro SD Cards (or storage in general)

I would suggest buying your micro SD cards in stores, instead of online marketplaces like AliExpress / Amazon / eBay / Temu / Wish / etc.

I say this because there are many fake micro SD cards online. “High Capacity” micro SD cards that are somehow cheaper than name brands. DO NOT trust these micro SD cards, they are a lower capacity than what they claim. You might already know this, but others do not. I've seen enough people try to use "512"GB micro SD cards to put out this notice.

Even if you could figure out the capacity, it’s still not a good idea to trust them, I feel as though this video is great at explaining why. It was made a few years ago, the information is still up-to-date in 2023!

Always stick to name brands like SanDisk. If you have to buy your micro SD card from a marketplace, make sure that you are buying from an official seller to reduce your chances of receiving a fake SD card. Really cheap micro SD cards can also fail easily so avoid any brands you have not heard of.

! > Sometimes stores like Walmart will sell some of their marketplace items in-store, if its high capacity and cheap, do not trust it.

If you are buying a cart, don't buy one with an included/pre-loaded micro SD card. These SD cards are made to the lowest standards possible, they will fail on you within weeks and all of your data will be gone. They are also more expensive than buying the cart on it's own. Save yourself the hassle and buy them on their own.

Recommended software

Here is the software that I would recommend installing before following the guide for your carts:

  • 7-Zip (Windows) - 7-Zip is a file archiver that is free and open source!
  • The Unarchiver (MacOS) - File extractor for Mac OS, much better than Archive Utility in my opinion!
  • PeaZip (Windows/MacOS/Linux) - Similar to 7-Zip, PeaZip is a file archiver that works on Windows, MacOS and Linux. While I prefer 7-Zip, PeaZip is also free and open source!
    PeaZip used to have an adware installer, it hasn't been that way for years now but I feel like it's something to take into consideration.

If you are using Linux, most of the time a file extractor will be included with your distro, I’d recommend using that instead.

! > I do not recommend using WinRAR - yes, it’s technically free as it doesn’t force you to pay - it isn't as good as some of the FOSS alternatives

Piracy Disclaimer

I have not, and will not, promote piracy on this site. This site is intended to set up your DS Flashcard, not to download ROMs. There are other sites dedicated to pirated ROMs.

Carts with included games

I don't recommend buying carts with included games. Not because the sellers are pirating ROMs to make a profit off of them. But because they come with shit SD cards (and are usually more expensive compared to carts without an SD card). See SD Cards for more information.

Timebomb Misconceptions

People tend to think that timebombs are some big scary malware which will brick your console and the cart when activated - that was a bit of an exaggeration but you get the point. In reality, they aren't harmful at all. You can read about them here: Timebomb Misconceptions
